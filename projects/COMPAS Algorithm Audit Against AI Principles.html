Project Overview

This project presents a comprehensive algorithmic audit of the COMPAS (Correctional Offender Management Profiling for Alternative Sanctions) system, which has been widely used in the U.S. criminal justice system to predict defendant's risk of recidivism. The audit examines racial disparities through multiple fairness metrics and evaluates the system against Google's AI Principles.

Tableau Python GitHub Fairness Metrics

Problem Statement

The COMPAS algorithm has been widely used in the U.S. criminal justice system to predict defendants' risk of recidivism. However, investigations have raised serious concerns about racial bias in its predictions, potentially leading to unfair sentencing decisions and perpetuating systemic inequalities.

Objective

Create an interactive audit dashboard that evaluates COMPAS for racial bias using multiple fairness metrics, examines disparities in False Positive Rates between racial groups, and provides data-driven recommendations based on Google's AI Principles.

Methodology & Process

Data Acquisition & Preparation

  • Sourced data from ProPublica's GitHub repository
  • Performed data cleaning and validation
  • Created calculated fields for fairness metrics
  • High Risk Flag (dynamic threshold)
  • False Positive / False Negative indicators
  • True Positive / True Negative indicators
  • False Positive Rate (FPR)
  • False Negative Rate (FNR)
  • Total Error Rate

Analysis Approach

  1. Demographic Overview: Established context of the dataset
  2. Bias Identification: Measured disparity in FPR between racial groups
  3. Control Analysis: Examined if disparities persist when controlling for prior arrests
  4. Trade-off Analysis: Explored relationship between FPR and FNR across groups
  5. Threshold Simulation: Created dynamic parameter to test different risk thresholds

Google's AI Principles Evaluation

Be socially beneficial

Evaluated whether COMPAS contributes positively to society or perpetuates existing biases in the criminal justice system.

Avoid creating or reinforcing unfair bias

Analyzed racial disparities in false positive rates and other fairness metrics to identify algorithmic bias.

Be built and tested for safety

Examined the robustness and safety of the COMPAS algorithm in real-world criminal justice applications.

Be accountable to people

Assessed the transparency and accountability mechanisms in place for COMPAS decision-making.

Incorporate privacy design principles

Evaluated privacy considerations in the data collection and processing practices of the COMPAS system.

Uphold high standards of scientific excellence

Reviewed the methodological rigor and validation approaches used in developing the COMPAS algorithm.

Be made available for uses that accord with these principles

Assessed whether COMPAS is deployed in ways consistent with ethical AI principles across different jurisdictions.

Key Findings

1

Significant Disparity in False Positive Rates

African-American defendants were almost twice as likely to be falsely labeled high risk compared to white defendants, indicating substantial racial bias in the algorithm's predictions.

2

Persistent Disparities Despite Controls

Disparities persisted even when controlling for prior arrests, suggesting that the algorithm's bias is not simply reflecting historical data but introducing new biases.

3

No Technical Solution to Eliminate Bias

No risk threshold eliminated the racial disparity in error rates, indicating fundamental flaws in the algorithm's design rather than simply incorrect calibration.

Business Impact

Criminal Justice System

Highlights need for reform in risk assessment tools and greater oversight of algorithmic decision-making in sentencing.

Policy Makers

Provides evidence for regulatory oversight of algorithms used in public sector applications, particularly in high-stakes domains.

Technology Companies

Demonstrates methodology for algorithmic auditing and the importance of building fairness considerations into AI systems from the ground up.

Actionable Recommendations

Immediate Actions

Suspend use of COMPAS for sentencing decisions until a thorough independent audit can be conducted and bias mitigation strategies implemented. Establish transparency requirements for all risk assessment tools used in criminal justice.

Medium-Term Solutions

Develop alternative algorithms with fairness constraints explicitly built into the model architecture. Create standardized evaluation frameworks for justice algorithms and implement ongoing bias testing and monitoring systems.

Long-Term Initiatives

Establish regulatory bodies for algorithmic oversight with authority to audit and certify algorithms used in high-stakes domains. Develop industry-wide standards for algorithmic fairness and invest in research on fair machine learning techniques.
